Pluggable Transports
keep your tools connected

Pluggable Transports are a concept developed by The Tor Project that provide ways to protect your data from aggressive censorship techniques, and can be included in a variety of tools.

We will be launching a broad, outward facing community here to expand the usage of transports and the community of implementers and developers.

How do I get involved?

Pluggable Transport Implementers Meetings

There was an event for current developers and implementers of transports, held in December 2015 in Washington, D.C.. This meeting focused on requirements gathering from the community to determine what would be useful in a revised Pluggable Transports specification.

A follow-up meeting was held in Valencia, Spain concurrent with the Internet Freedom Festival. This meeting was a workshop for reviewing proposals for improvement of the Pluggable Transport specification. Three proposals were reviewed:

  • Pluggable Transports Go API
  • Pluggable Transport UDP Support Proposal
  • Pluggable Transports Work In Progress

Taking the feedback from the Valencia meeting, the three proposals were merged to create a new draft specification, Pluggable Transports Specification, Version 2, Draft 1. This draft specification is based on the PT 1.0 specification and the Pluggable Transports Work in Progress. It also contains the Pluggable Transports Go API and Pluggable Transport UDP Support. Additionally, it includes feedback from the workshop on those proposals. This draft specification has been circulated to the Pluggable Transport and circumvention tool developers that attended the implementers meetings.

Pluggable Transports Small Grants Fund

Following the Pluggable Transport Implementers Meetings, a request for proposals was sent out for small grants to improve on the state of Pluggable Transports. These grants are intended to support both the creation of new transports and integration of Pluggable Transports into existing applications.

Applications for the small grants fund are now closed, but the Request for Applications (RFA) can be found here for reference.

Resources

Please find below a wealth of existing community resources

  • obfuscation.github.io

    obfuscation.github.io provides a central point for existing PT implementers to collaborate with a bi-weekly chat and email group

  • Resources

    The Tor Project has a page with a list of current transports, sample libraries, and upcoming concepts.

  • Mobile Work

    The Guardian Project's Orbot enables censorship circumvention for Android phones, and their Pluto library specifically implements pluggable transports.

  • Documentation

    The Tor Project has a large documentation repository and specs. A Child's Garden on Pluggable Transports shows what transports look like on the wire. StackExchange on Pluggable Transports has additional Q&A style resources.
